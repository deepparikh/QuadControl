ESC32 - "fried" then short circuit

by Omnitoto » Fri Oct 26, 2012 8:08 pm

Tonight, after finishing assembling all ESC32s and thoroughly testing one successfully I decided to give a try plugging all 4 to the AQ to check that all esc were properly power supplied.

At first the 4 escs were flashing red as expected but then I started to hear some frying noises. I disconnected everything and found 1 of the pwm cable connecting escs quite hot as well as the dc converter on the AQ.
Following this, there is now a short circuit on the esc at least on power supply from pwm cable or ftdi. I haven't tried main power supply.

Nothing seems burnt on the esc and i yet didn't investigated more about it as i was busy all evening making sure the AQ was ok which at first didn't look pretty well but in the end everything on the AQ seems back to normal.


If anyone of you has an idea on what could be the root cause of the short circuit that could be a good help when i start to investigate if i can recover it.

Thank you,

Re: ESC32 - "fried" then short circuit

by Omnitoto » Sun Oct 28, 2012 12:01 am

I found what seems to be the problem and it has affected 2 of my 4 esc so far.

There is a very small trace of ground running below the 5V pin of the FTDI connector close to the edge of the board and it has been able to shortcut the supply power from this connector.

Powering ESC from the main power supply seems to be a better or safer option for as I had the problem on 2 ESCs and not from the very beginning.
